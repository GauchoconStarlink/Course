📈 The Pullback Setup

Class 33: Buying the Dip in a Strong Uptrend (Continuation)
Phase 7 · Strategies & Edges · Crypto · Trend-continuation

📚 What It Is & Why It Works

The pullback is the trend-continuation trade: instead of chasing a breakout, you buy a temporary dip inside a strong uptrend — at a spot where support stacks up (rising moving averages + a prior level) — with a tight stop just below. It's the signature setup of U.S. Investing Champion Martin Luk, and the whole edge is risk as little as possible for maximum upside.

Luk's numbers say it all: a low win rate (~23%) but tiny average losses (~3%) and occasional huge winners — because his stops are so tight (often 1–4%), a normal winner becomes 10R, 20R, even 30R. You don't need to be right often; you need to be right big while losing small. That's positive expectancy, not prediction.

"Buy weakness in strength — not weakness in weakness."
— Martin Luk

The psychology — why the dip gets bought

In a strong uptrend, everyone who bought the move is in profit and wants to add on a dip; institutions accumulate on pullbacks toward the rising averages. When price pulls back into that support:

  • Weak, late buyers get shaken out on the dip (their stops sit just under support).
  • The rising MAs act as a magnet/floor — the average buyer since the move began is roughly break-even there, so they defend it.
  • When price breaks back above the prior day's high, it proves the sellers are done and buyers are back in control — and the trend resumes with a fresh wave of dip-buyers and add-ons.

You're not calling a top or bottom — you're joining an already-proven uptrend at the one moment it offers a low-risk entry.

How it fits your playbook: VCP = breakout from a tight base. RDR = counter-trend reversal after a drop. The Pullback = with-trend continuation — buy the dip in a coin that's already trending up. Read Bitcoin first, then pick the tool that fits the condition.

🟢 The Setup — Step by Step

✅ Setup Requirements — everything that must be true before you take the trade

  • Bitcoin / market is bullish — the gate. Pullbacks only continue in uptrends.
  • Coin is a leader — outperforming Bitcoin, making higher highs & higher lows.
  • EMAs stacked & rising: 9 EMA > 21 EMA > 50 EMA, all sloping up. (This is Luk's "Lead" condition.)
  • High volatility / ADR — the coin actually moves, so a winner pays for many small losers.
  • Orderly pullback into the rising 9/21 EMA zone — a few quiet candles, not a high-volume collapse.
  • Pullback tightens into a quiet, small-range setup day (ideally an inside day) sitting on the EMAs.
  • Trigger ready: you have marked the high of that setup day — that is your entry line.
  • Stop defined: the low of the setup day — and it must be tight (≤ ~3.5%, and no more than half the coin's daily range). If it's wider, skip or size down.
  • Risk ≤ 0.5–1% of account on the trade.

The five steps

  1. Strong coin only. A leader in a clear uptrend — up big over recent weeks/months, higher highs & higher lows, trading above rising 9/21/50 EMAs (stacked 9>21>50), outperforming Bitcoin. Never buy dips in weak names.
  2. Let it pull back into the EMAs. After a run, price eases back a few candles toward the rising 9/21 EMA — an orderly dip, not a violent collapse. This is the "buy zone."
  3. Wait for the dip to tighten into a setup day. The selling dries up and price prints a quiet, small-range candle (often an inside day) sitting on the rising EMAs. This setup day is your reference candle — mark its high and its low.
  4. Enter on the break of the setup day's high. The moment price trades above the high of that setup day, the pullback is over and the trend is resuming — that break is your entry. You do not buy while price is still falling. (Full detail on the "Entry & Stop" tab.)
  5. Stop at the setup-day low. Place the stop just under the setup day's low — tight. That tight stop is what turns a normal winner into a big multiple of your risk.

Why the setup-day high — not a moving average? Martin Luk's actual trigger is the break of the prior day's high (or opening-range high) after a pullback tightens on the EMAs. The 9/21/50 EMAs tell you where to hunt (the buy zone and the trend); the setup-day high is the exact line that gets you in. That's what makes the entry mechanical instead of a guess.

🎯 Entry & Stop

The one rule to be crystal clear on: the trigger is NOT "when price touches or reclaims a moving average." The trigger is the break of the high of the setup day — the tight, quiet candle that formed when the pullback stopped on the rising 9/21 EMA. The EMAs define the zone; the setup-day high is the exact price that puts you in.

Entry — step by step

  1. Identify the setup day. After the pullback into the rising 9/21 EMA, wait for a quiet, small-range candle (ideally an inside day — its high & low sit inside the previous candle's range). That is your setup / reference day.
  2. Mark its high. The high of the setup day = your trigger line. Draw it on the chart (green line in the picture).
  3. Enter on the break of that high. The moment price trades above the setup-day high, you're in. That is the entry — Martin Luk's exact trigger is the break of the prior day's high (or the intraday/opening-range high) once the pullback has tightened.
  4. Don't chase. If price is already well above the setup-day high before you got the break, let it go — the low-risk entry is gone. Wait for the next setup.

Standard entry (what we teach)

Buy the break of the setup-day high as it happens. Simple, mechanical, and the stop (setup-day low) is right there — so risk is tiny.

More conservative

Wait for the candle to close above the setup-day high before entering. Fewer fakeouts from a wick, slightly worse price and a bit more risk. Use this if crypto's wicks keep tricking you.

Stop

  • Stop = just below the setup-day low (the low of that quiet reference candle). Nothing else. It's marked before you enter, so your risk is defined to the cent.
  • It must be tight: aim for ≤ ~3.5%, and never more than half the coin's daily range (ADR). If the setup-day low is further than that, the trade is too loose — skip it or size down.
  • "Winning horses don't back up." Once triggered, a good pullback shouldn't come back and revisit the setup-day low. If it does, the entry was wrong — take the small loss without argument.

Position size & risk

  • Risk 0.5–1% of your account per trade (Luk keeps it here). Because the stop is so tight, you can still take a meaningful position while risking very little in dollars.
  • Tight stop = big R. A 25% winner on a 3% stop ≈ 8R; on a 1.5% stop ≈ 16R. The tight setup-day stop is the entire edge — that's why the precise entry matters.

Crypto note: only hunt these when Bitcoin is bullish and the coin is a leader vs BTC. Because crypto wicks levels, if you keep getting faked out use the conservative "close above the setup-day high" version, and keep any leverage well away from liquidation.

🛠️ Exits

  • Sell into strength: trim a piece into a sharp push (e.g., at 3R+ or into an obvious resistance) so you bank the move.
  • Trail the rest: use the 9 EMA as a trailing stop in fast moves, the 21 or 50 EMA in slower/stronger trends — exit when price closes below your chosen trail.
  • Structure: also exit if the trend breaks (a clear lower high / lower low) or the setup's support gives way.

Match the exit to the move: in a rip, take more into strength; in a steady grind, trail wider (21/50 EMA) and give it room. The goal is to cut losers tiny and let a few winners run into big R.

🔎 Filters & Confluence

  • Bitcoin / market bullish — the gate. Pullbacks continue in uptrends, not downtrends.
  • Leader outperforming BTC — buy dips in the strongest names, not laggards.
  • Above rising 9/21/50 EMAs, higher highs & higher lows.
  • Orderly pullback into support — not a high-volume collapse.
  • Dip holds the 9/21 EMA zone — the pullback stalls on the rising EMAs (bonus if a prior level/gap or AVWAP lines up there too).
  • Tight setup day — the dip tightens into a quiet, small-range candle (ideally an inside day) on the EMAs.
  • Trigger = break of the setup-day high — that break is the entry; stop at the setup-day low.

Optional level-up: Anchored VWAP

AVWAP is a support line you pin to the candle where the move began (no length to set — just the anchor). It shows the average price paid since then, so buyers defend it. Anchor it to the swing low that started the run; if a pullback reclaims it alongside the 9/21 EMA, that's A+ confluence. It is an optional bonus — the setup works on the EMAs and support alone.

🎯 Key Takeaways

  1. Buy weakness in strength — a dip inside a proven uptrend, never a falling knife.
  2. Enter on the break of the setup-day high after the dip holds the rising 9/21 EMA; stop at the setup-day low — tight.
  3. Tight stop = big R. Low win rate is fine when winners are 10–30× the small risk (Martin Luk's edge).
  4. Risk 0.5–1%, don't chase, cut fast if it revisits the setup-day low ("winning horses don't back up").
  5. Exit: trim into strength, trail with the 9 EMA (21/50 in stronger trends).
  6. Trend-first: only in bullish markets, in leaders outperforming Bitcoin.

Method: the pullback approach of Martin Luk (U.S. Investing Championship), applied to crypto.
